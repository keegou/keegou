Ten Months
What ten months can hold — and what they release.

Ten months.

Not long. Not short. Just enough for something to change without you noticing it changing.

——

Ten months ago, I made a decision. It wasn't dramatic. I didn't announce it. I just turned a small handle in my mind, and started walking in a slightly different direction.

At first, nothing felt different. Day one felt like day zero. Week one felt like a performance. I was doing the thing, but I wasn't the thing yet.

——

Then somewhere around month three, something shifted. Not in the result—in the effort. The thing I was forcing started to feel less like forcing. The voice that said "I have to" started to sound more like "I can."

Month five: I stopped checking progress. I just did it.

Month seven: Someone asked me how I started. I didn't know what to say. I couldn't point to a single moment. It had just… settled.

——

Ten months is long enough for a habit to move from your head into your bones. Long enough for a thought you borrowed from someone else to become yours.

I used to think change required a crisis. A rock bottom. A dramatic break. Now I think change requires ten months, a small direction, and the willingness to not check results every week.

——

What are you ten months into?

Or maybe: what would you be willing to carry for ten months, without knowing where it leads?

You don't need to answer now. Just hold the question.
