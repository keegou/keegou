I Didn't Tell Anyone

On carrying something alone, and the quiet way it changes you.

$5 · Pay What You Want

I didn't tell anyone. Not because I was hiding it. Not because I was ashamed. Just because I didn't know how to say it in a way that would make sense.

It was a decision I had made — a small one, but one that would quietly change the direction of things. And every day, I got up and worked on it. Not in a dramatic way. Not in a "I'm chasing my dream" way. Just in a "I have this thing I need to do, and no one knows about it" way.

——

There is something strange about carrying a secret that is not a secret — it's not something I was hiding from the world. It was something I was simply not sharing yet. The difference is subtle but important. A secret wants to stay hidden. This thing was just — not ready.

——

The days passed. I kept working. Some days I felt proud of what I was building. Other days I wondered if it was all a waste of time. But the hardest part wasn't the doubt. It was the isolation.

You are the only audience for both the triumph and the failure. And that is a lonely place to be.
